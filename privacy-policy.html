Flamme — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2, 2026

Flamme is a personal recipe app. It was built to keep your data on your device, so this policy is short.

The short version

Flamme does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. Everything you put into the app stays on your phone.

What the app stores, and where

Your recipes, photos, ratings, shopping list, meal plans, cooking plans, and settings are saved only in the app's private storage on your device. They are never uploaded anywhere. We (the developer) cannot see them.

If you delete the app, that data is deleted with it. You can also erase everything yourself at any time by clearing the app's storage in Android settings.

Internet access

Flamme works fully offline. The app connects to the internet in exactly one situation: when you paste a link to import a recipe, the app fetches that web page (the same as your browser would) to read the recipe. Nothing about you is sent along, and nothing is sent anywhere else — no analytics, no advertising networks, no crash reporting, no third-party services.

Backups

The Backup feature writes your data to a single file and hands it to you through Android's share screen. Where that file goes (your Drive, your email, a cable to your computer) is entirely your choice and under your control. Flamme never sends it anywhere on its own.

Photos

When you attach a photo to a recipe, Android's photo picker gives the app a copy of just that photo, which is stored with your recipes on your device. The app cannot browse your photo library.

Permissions

Flamme requests no sensitive permissions. It cannot use your camera, microphone, or location.

Children

Flamme does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of Flamme ever changes how data is handled (for example, an optional cloud-sync feature), this policy will be updated first and the change will be clearly announced in the app's update notes.
